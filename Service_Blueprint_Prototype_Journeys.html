Journey NarrativeWhat is happening in this moment
A weekend morning after breakfast. Nova sits with Mila on the couch; Tom tidies the kitchen. Mila is about to start school in a few months. Her room is still a toddler room — house bed, toy shelf, no desk. Nova opens OTTO and taps the microphone.
Nova explains what they need: a desk, a proper workspace, a bigger bed, more storage. The cube shelf stays, everything else can change. “And Mila is here and wants to help decide.” OTTO recognises the pattern: a full room redesign where almost everything is rethought. It responds: “That sounds like an exciting project. Hello Mila, your first big room upgrade!”
OTTO asks for photos. Nova takes two: entrance view showing bed and window, opposite wall with the cube shelf. OTTO turns to Mila: “What do you like doing most in your room?” Mila leans to the mic: “Drawing! And listening to stories. And my fox pillow lives there.” OTTO analyses the photos and cross-references Nova’s order history, identifying the birch house bed and cube shelf from four years ago.
The Stylefinder activates with large visual tiles: “Underwater World” in soft blues with dolphins and shells, “Forest Adventure” in warm greens and browns, “Northern Lights” in cool pastels. Mila taps “Underwater World” immediately: “That one! With the dolphins!” She adds “Northern Lights”: “And the bright one. But no baby pink.” Nova and Tom each add their picks. OTTO registers the family overlap: water themes + clean lines.
The screen transforms into a project canvas: room photo divided into three zones (sleep, work/creative, play/reading). Below: a 16-item checklist covering furniture, textiles, workspace tools, learning toys, and décor. Tom reads it: “The desk organiser and the pinboard — clever. She’ll need those from day one.” OTTO suggests a tablet for later; they defer it to the wishlist. Budget: €1,800.
Three complete room concepts rendered in Mila’s actual room. “Marine Explorer”: mid-height bed with reading cave beneath, ocean-blue palette. “Creative Coast”: tilting desk and large pinboard, coastal warmth. “Northern Light by the Sea”: solid oak furniture, calm ice-blue tones, headboard bookshelf. All €1,890–1,980, all integrate the existing cube shelf.
OTTO shows Mila a child-friendly view: big images, heart buttons. Mila loves the reading cave from “Marine Explorer”, the pinboard from “Creative Coast”, and the headboard bookshelf from “Northern Light.” Tom worries: “And when she’s twelve? Will she still climb a ladder?” OTTO mediates: the oak bed from Northern Light grows with Mila, plus a marine canopy in light blue gives the cave feeling without the constraints. “When she’s older, simply remove the canopy.” Mila: “Yes! Like sleeping underwater!”
OTTO builds the hybrid: oak bed with marine canopy, Northern Light desk, pinboard, blue bedding sets from the Coast concept. Fourteen items total. Rendered in Mila’s room: bed on the left wall, canopy in light blue, fox pillow on the bed, desk by the window with pinboard above, cube shelf repurposed as book tower, wave-pattern wool rug. Mila points at the screen: “There’s my fox pillow!” — the first thing she recognises in her new room.
Nova taps the desk: OTTO shows dimensions and a height-adjustment video (six-year-old height → ten-year-old height). Cable channel keeps cords hidden. OTTO suggests a child-safe power strip with USB ports (€19) for the cable channel. Tom activates it immediately. Mila taps the pinboard: “Can I hang photos of my friends?” “Yes! Cork surface for pins, and magnetic strips for photos without holes.” Mila beams.
“Let’s sleep on it,” says Nova. OTTO saves the complete project. A few days later: Mila tells her friend Lina about the new room. Lina has fairy lights over her bed. “Mama, can I have those too?” That evening Nova reopens OTTO. The project is exactly as they left it. She finds a warm-white micro-LED string perfect for the marine canopy and adds it.
The following weekend. Nova and Tom review everything together. Project summary grouped by function: furniture, textiles, workspace, décor, plus the LED string. Fifteen new items + the existing cube shelf. Total after 5% bundle discount: €1,793. Under budget. They check out: four monthly installments, coordinated delivery in three weeks. OTTO offers assembly for bed and desk. Tom books it before Nova can answer.
Three weeks later, the room is ready. Mila climbs onto the bed, looks up into the marine canopy, and whispers: “Like underwater.” She puts her fox pillow at the headboard, sits at the desk, draws a dolphin with crayons, and pins it to the pinboard as the very first thing. On the first day of school, a timetable hangs next to it.

Journey NarrativeWhat is happening in this moment
Leonie’s old two-seater is the last thing in her carefully curated flat that doesn’t belong. She opens OTTO and types “Sofa.” OTTO presents curated options and an interactive moodboard built from her purchase history.
She browses, saves favourites, swipes mismatches away. With each interaction the suggestions improve. It gets late; she goes to bed. Next day on the commute: OTTO greets her with the moodboard updated overnight.
OTTO senses she’s ready to narrow down and starts asking short questions between stops. “How many people?” — “Usually just me, sometimes two or three.” “Firm or deep?” — “Deep. I like to curl up.” “Style?” — “Warm tones, round shapes, texture. No leather.” “It needs to work as a guest bed for my sister.” Then: “I noticed you ordered dog food, a harness, and a dog bed. Do you have a dog?” She glances at Bruno, her two-year-old Labrador. “He sits on the sofa more than I do.” Budget: up to €1,500.
With each exchange the moodboard narrows. From dozens to a handful that truly fit: sleeper function, dog-friendly fabric, warm tones, round shapes, right budget. The Generative UI shows a curated selection with smart icons highlighting what matters to Leonie. Six results visible, “Show more” below. No FOMO, full control. She picks three favourites.
OTTO suggests: “Want to see them in your living room?” She’s on the go, so she waits. Later at home, OTTO picks up seamlessly. She takes two photos: entrance with sideboard and walnut table, window looking back into the room. OTTO renders all three sofas in her space — as OTTO-Up customer, in high resolution with her room’s actual lighting. The sofas look like they’re already there.
She swipes between the three for a minute. “The second — the grey and metal legs don’t fit; everything here is wood. The third is beautiful but too small. The first is closest, but the backrest should be higher. Does it come with dark wood legs?” OTTO searches the catalogue: two new options, each rendered. One refines the first (higher backrest, walnut legs). One goes in a different direction within her palette (softer feel, solid walnut legs). Both in budget.
Side by side. The updated first sofa looks like it was designed for her space. Walnut legs ground it, higher backrest looks proportional. The alternative is appealing but less distinctive. She chooses the first. Decision made.
She’s never touched this fabric. She taps the swatch: a full-screen rotating close-up under studio lighting. Material profile below: “Flat-woven structured cotton, dense binding. Firm and smooth, linen-like quality. Honey tone is yarn-dyed — won’t fade from the courtyard window light. Pilling resistance 5/5.” She imagines Bruno kneading the fabric and the dense weave holding up.
The sleeper mechanism. A fifteen-second AI-generated clip: someone pulls the base forward in one motion, the integrated topper folds flat. No loose parts, no cushions to remove. Second clip: pushed back in under ten seconds. “That’s it? You just pull it out?” She watches twice and pictures the evening before Jule arrives.
OTTO suggests an amber throw (€39): “A washable layer for Bruno’s favourite spot. Protects the upholstery, machine-washable at 40 °C.” Added without hesitation. Product summary: sofa rendered in her room on top, details below — product, sleeper, throw, total price, a personal note about why this fits her daily life with Bruno and Jule.
“Want to redeem your OTTO-Up points?” She redeems them. Checkout in under a minute: three installments, delivery with placement in a few days, old sofa disposal included. She puts the phone down and scratches Bruno behind the ears. “You’re getting an upgrade too.”
A few days later, the sofa arrives. That evening she sends Jule a photo of the unfolded guest bed: “No more air mattress. Come whenever you want.” Jule books a visit. The next morning: legs tucked up, coffee on the side table, courtyard light streaming in. Bruno curled on the amber throw at the other end, exactly where she knew he’d be. The room looks like she imagined it.
